Text Editor

The "Text Editor" tab is located in the upper part in the middle of the "Corpus" work space. The text input field is empty if no text from the Corpora is selected. Upon selecting the text you will be able to transliterate it and enter the hyeroglyphs. This chapter provides the general description of the tab. For a step-by-step instruction of how to enter a new text please refer to the relevant chapter in the "How-to" section of this manual.

The "Text Editor" window contains two input fields. The upper one displays either "Transliteration", "Sign Text Editor" or "JSesh View" - depending on the tab selected. The lower input field displays translation of the sentence, selected in the upper window.

Transliteration tab

Here you can enter or modify the transliteration of the text. Be careful to follow the BTS Grammar Reference, otherwise the autochecker system will trigger an error. The "Transliteration" tab is semantically connected to the Lemmatizer tab below, where the transliterated words are referenced to individual lemmata.

Sign Text Editor tab

The "Sign-Text-Editor" tab provides the hieroglyphic version of the text. Initially only the transliteration is displayed there - each word / lemma in a separate box. The hieroglyphic input is done in the "Hieroglyph Type Writer" tab below. The hieroglyphic values, entered there, are automatically applied to the selected lemmas in the "Sign-Text-Editor" window.

JSesh View

Displays a cohesive hieroglyphic text.

There are several buttons above the "Text-Editor" window:

"Load Lemmata of Text" loads the word borders and the sentence borders of your texts in order to be able to add a translation and to lemmatize the words in the next step.

"Check Text Lemmata, Flexcodes..." checks the completeness of text lemmatization, translation and hieroglyphic transcription.

"Add Annotation" adds the Annotation to the selected part of your text. It will be underlined with a black dotted line.

"Add Rubrum" converts the active lemma into a rubrum, which is marked red in the text.

"Add Subtext/Glosse" adds a subtext or a gloss respectively as a "child" of your text. You can edit it separately.

"Add Comment" adds a comment to the selected part. It will be underlined yellow.

You can use these functions also via the drop-down menu that appears when you click on the arrow on the right.

Additionally, for Annotations, Rubra and Comments, a small, rectangular bar will appear on the right side of the Text-Editor with the corresponding colour. Clicking on the bar will bring you to the marked fragment of the text.

"Sentence translation" text input field

At the bottom of the "Text Editor" window there is the text-input field, where you can enter your translation. It is empty unless a lemma has been selected in the "Text editor" window.

"Languages" icon offers a drop-down with a number of languages ( English, German, French, Spanish, Russian) to choose from. Choose the language of your translation.
