Grammar check

The BTS is capable of checking a transliteration for errors by using system grammar rules. This does not mean that it can recognize false lemmata or transcription errors and correct them. In fact, it follows the internal logic according to which specific signs can only occur in specific combinations. It detects disagreements with this logic and marks them:

Images/Ansicht_mit_Fehlern.jpg

Errors are marked by a dark red encircled rectangle on the right next to the scroll bar of the Texteditor. There are also light red encircled rectangles. These show the location of a rubrum (in this example l.19 and l.37). A red wavy line in the transliteration field marks errors in the Texteditor window itself. On mouse-over at the red square on the left of the Texteditor you can see a ToolTip with information about the cause of the error.

Images/Ansicht_mit_Fehlern_on_mouse_over.jpg

In this case a hyphen is missing in front of the line-end marker "§". If you insert the required symbol the error message will disappear:
